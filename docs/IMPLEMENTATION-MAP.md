## Empty-handling coverage rule

Empty semantics is not complete at field-kind granularity. Coverage is clause-shaped and must record field kind, operator and operand position, enclosing consumer, selection/all-empty identity, model-derived field role, row eligibility, validation/computation phase, and observable polarity or store outcome. The current ┬¦2 capsule covers only direct Number/Boolean/Confirm field-to-literal comparison and presence behavior; its resolvers must not be reused as defaults for functions, aggregates, lookups, or computation. [`LF5`](LEAN-FINDINGS.md#lf5--empty-handling-is-a-layered-consuming-clause-policy-not-a-field-kind-function) records the verified counterexamples and architectural treatment.

## Current external-evidence gate

The retained kernel 30.8.1 bundle and typed Lean projections now replay 30 focused observations through `lake test`: 28 runtime cases and two static diagnostics. Empty/branch behavior, absolute requiredness, explicit paths, the corrected two-tier bare-name mechanism, uncorrelated 5a selection, and captured-outer 5b direction/self-match/self-exclusion all have separating evidence. The three standalone models behind the four 5b cases retain the actual executed stored conditions containing `$`; the typed projection does not trust a formatter or conversion round-trip. This closes 5a's narrow one-group truth capsule and the four stated clean-filled 5b shapes, without extending the external claim to other admitted correlated operator/state combinations, authored repeatable elaboration, diagnostics, polarity, general consumers, or any other excluded axis. The legal arithmetic precision witness remains open until arithmetic makes it authorable. New capture may run through the external a12-dmkits adapter harness only while keeping the local `a12-rulekit/` checkout's tracked and visible worktree clean; tracked corpus generation must target an ignored disposable workspace before portable artifacts are copied here. Internal examples remain distinct from differential evidence.
