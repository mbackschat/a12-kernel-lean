## Preserved claims and deliberate exclusions

The compact 13-case record preserves direct copy, suffix concatenation, two-field concatenation, clean empty contribution, final-empty no-store, changed/equal/stale/absent target deltas, and content-bearing versus empty-row controls. Both kernel routes agreed. The historical a12-dmkits interpreter disagreed only on the three all-empty final-store cases by reporting empty `VALUE`; that discrepancy was later confirmed and fixed upstream as IF123. It remains historical triangulation, not the oracle or an active replay channel.

The compact nine-case record preserves positive minimum/maximum checks, inclusive boundaries, accepted equality, payloadful `tooShort`/`tooLong` errors, padded spaces, change-sensitive delta, and exact accepted/error application over absent and filled priors. Both kernel routes agreed; a12-dmkits agreed on projected delta and stored-value application while its historical view omitted the target-error cause and collapsed exact empty placement.

Neither record establishes intermediate expression terms, poison, initially present-empty input, line-break permission, general Unicode beyond the retained conservative text, scheduling, alternatives, repeatable scope, document traversal, or public protocol support. Those remain internal laws, open boundaries, or future evidence questions as recorded by the living implementation map.
