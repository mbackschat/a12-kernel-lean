# CLAUDE.md

Guidance for working in **a12-kernel-lean** — a clean-room, versioned **Lean 4 formal theory and executable specification** of the A12 kernel's validation & computation semantics.

## What this is

A12 Kernel is mgm technology partners' model-and-DSL engine for complex business forms: business analysts declare **validation rules** (each states the *error* condition — true ⇒ the data is invalid) and **computations** (derived fields) in a bilingual EN/DE DSL, and the engine evaluates them against form *Documents*. This project preserves that observed **evaluation semantics** as a versioned mechanized theory: executable as a reference oracle, empirically anchored to kernel 30.8.1, equipped with a required proof spine plus selectively proved higher-level properties, and designed to produce purpose-specific semantic shipments for independent evaluators and later checked consumers such as importers and rule-refactoring tools.

The project-owned language-neutral semantics live in [`spec/`](spec/) — start at [`spec/SEMANTICS-MAP.md`](spec/SEMANTICS-MAP.md) and follow the numbered deep-dives. They are the semantic bridge to a12-dmkits. [`docs/README.md`](docs/README.md) is the sole registry and ownership map for the living project documentation; do not reproduce its complete catalog here.

- **Extend semantics:** start from the canonical clause in [`spec/`](spec/), use [`docs/SOURCES.md`](docs/SOURCES.md) for its provenance and drill path, record every behavioral spec change in the same change under [`docs/A12-DMKITS-SPEC-SYNC.md`](docs/A12-DMKITS-SPEC-SYNC.md), inspect current clause/proof/evidence coverage in [`docs/IMPLEMENTATION-MAP.md`](docs/IMPLEMENTATION-MAP.md), and follow the capsule workflow in [`docs/TESTING.md`](docs/TESTING.md). [`spec/13-lean-encoding-guide.md`](spec/13-lean-encoding-guide.md) remains consulted staged guidance, not a writable plan.
- **Resume current work:** read [`docs/PLAN.md`](docs/PLAN.md) for the verified baseline summary, current spiral, and stop conditions; follow its links to the owning evidence and capability records.
- **Explore a consumer:** start with the bounded probe catalog in [`docs/USE-CASES.md`](docs/USE-CASES.md), then use [`docs/IMPLEMENTER-GUIDE.md`](docs/IMPLEMENTER-GUIDE.md) for the task profile, probe boundary, semantic shipments, and independent-consumer gates. Use [`docs/PROTOCOL.md`](docs/PROTOCOL.md) only when the selected task actually consumes the normalized reference process.

## Goal & role

**A versioned mechanized theory, executable-first, with a required proof spine and additional proofs selected by payoff** (decided 2026-07-12). Build small semantic capsules: each lands with its executable clause, useful law when one exists, nearest checked non-law, exact assumptions, coverage entry, and honest external-evidence status. Exercise closed material through bounded consumer probes across the Execute/Translate/Transform/Compile/Analyze/Verify/Synthesize/Qualify/Explain/Govern taxonomy owned by [`docs/PRODUCT-PROPOSAL.md`](docs/PRODUCT-PROPOSAL.md#general-consumer-task-categories) and explained for users in [`docs/USE-CASES.md`](docs/USE-CASES.md), then promote only explicitly adopted, research-closed capabilities into versioned purpose-specific semantic shipments. Independent evaluators are the first consumer profile, while selected external-model importers and A12 rule-refactoring tools are later concrete examples with their own source/target fragments and preservation obligations. Related semantic families are differentially calibrated against compact retained observations from the real engine. The accepted direct-cascade raw capture and qualification are historical a12-dmkits artifacts retained for audit; the unused portable capture V1 producer was retired from current upstream `main`, and its packet machinery is not reimplemented inside Lean. The sibling corpus is knowledge and source material, not a second live replay dependency. Proofs establish internal universal consequences and semantic-preservation bridges, never universal correspondence to the external kernel or automatic correctness of an independent consumer. Lean is the ecosystem's formal semantics-of-record for the chosen account of observed behaviour — *not* a replacement for the shipped Kotlin interpreter. Read [`docs/PROJECT-DESIGN.md`](docs/PROJECT-DESIGN.md) for the charter and [`docs/LEAN-FORMALIZATION.md`](docs/LEAN-FORMALIZATION.md) before extending the proof or semantics architecture; read [`docs/ARCHITECTURE.md`](docs/ARCHITECTURE.md) before extending the core types.

## Semantic-first simplification rule

The default unit of progress is an A12 semantic clause with compact conformance cases and payoff-selected laws, not a new evidence or process layer. Reuse an existing type only when its current meaning fits exactly. Apply a live-value test before retaining non-semantic code: name its current consumer, the unique invariant it enforces, and why a smaller existing mechanism cannot enforce that invariant. If any answer is missing, consolidate or delete it. Previous implementation cost, historical reproducibility, and possible future reuse are not live value; preserve completed outcomes as compact immutable artifacts and exact Git revision pointers instead of runnable legacy machinery.

The default Tier 1 capsule budget is one semantic module, one conformance module, one proof module, small updates to existing document owners, at most 250 new nonblank Lean lines, and zero new IO executables, schemas, receipts, registries, generators, or packet binders. The line target is a decomposition signal, not permission to compress readable code: exceed it only after splitting by semantic responsibility or recording why the semantic mechanism is genuinely indivisible.

A capsule may land as `external evidence pending`. Batch related external calibration at semantic milestones and consume compact retained observations or a newly accepted purpose-specific handback; do not assume a standing producer contract. Build cross-family infrastructure once at a stable boundary and then treat it as settled. Reopen infrastructure only when an actual consumer is blocked or a second semantic family demonstrates the same unmet need; never for anticipated reuse. New shared infrastructure requires a demonstrated repeated need that the settled compact path cannot serve, a named semantic consumer, and an explicit deletion or replacement plan for superseded code.

Apply a live-value test before retaining process or evidence source: it stays only when current semantics or a current consumer depends on it, it guards a failure class not already owned by a smaller settled mechanism, or it is needed to reproduce non-frozen current state. Immutable evidence and historical reports are not live source consumers. Once their exact bytes and last reproducing revision are recorded and a current gate pins the accepted artifacts, remove completed one-off generators, adapters, packet binders, differential campaigns, and qualification runners. Git history is the reproduction archive; prior effort and possible future convenience are not retention arguments.

Once [`docs/PLAN.md`](docs/PLAN.md) marks the bounded simplification wave complete, treat the common evidence and process boundary as settled and resume from its first unfinished semantic step. Reopen infrastructure only for a reproduced defect in a retained shared mechanism, a genuinely new observation shape required by an adopted semantic family, or a concrete shipment/consumer gate. Routine capsules add no process format or qualification layer. If capsule-specific support code approaches or exceeds its semantics, proofs, and conformance code, stop and redesign around the compact shared path before continuing.

The repository-level ratio is also a guard. Count `Core.lean`, `Cell.lean`, `Document.lean`, `Basic.lean`, the `Semantics/`, `Elaboration/`, `Proofs/`, and `Conformance/` trees, and their library roots as the executable theory estate. Count the `Evidence/`, `Reference/`, and `Process/` trees plus their IO/test drivers as the support estate. Support must not exceed theory in tracked nonblank Lean lines, and evidence alone should remain below one third of theory. Crossing either threshold requires stopping, reporting the concrete consumer and unique invariant causing it, and redesigning or obtaining an explicit owner exception before adding more support code. The completed count method and migration measurements remain in the [archived simplification work program](docs/archived/REFERENCE-AND-EVIDENCE-SIMPLIFICATION-PROPOSAL.md); the live scaling rule and settled baseline are owned by the [`compact semantic-evidence pipeline`](docs/SEMANTIC-CAPSULE-PIPELINE-PROPOSAL.md).

## ⚠️ HARD RULE — clean-room reimplementation; never link, call, or transcribe the kernel

This is a **licensing boundary, not a preference**, and it is the same rule the a12-dmkits interpreter already operates under. The A12 kernel is **EUPL-1.2 (copyleft) OR commercial**. A work that *links, ships, or calls* the kernel is a combined work the EUPL copyleft reaches; a **line-by-line transliteration of its source** is a copyright derivative that re-attracts the EUPL. Therefore:

- **Never** link, call, or ship the kernel (or a JS-kernel binding) from this project, and **never** transcribe a kernel *source expression* into Lean.
- **Do** read the kernel — and the a12-dmkits interpreter under `../a12-rulekit/interpreter/` — to *learn the exact behaviour*, then write **original** Lean and lock it against that behaviour with property/differential tests. Reading the source to *understand* (and to find surprising special cases) is encouraged; the *fix* is never a transliteration. **Copy the mechanism, never the expression.**

Because this project's source ships no kernel code, it is licensed **MIT** (see [`LICENSE`](LICENSE)) — matching the A12 OSS family (a12-dmkits source is MIT for the same reason; see [`../a12-rulekit/docs/LICENSING.md`](../a12-rulekit/docs/LICENSING.md)).

## ⚠️ HARD RULE — semantics first; harness, governance, and redundant work require explicit approval

The default work in this repository is A12 semantics: executable definitions, red/green conformance examples, useful theorems and counterexamples, and the minimum same-change spec, status, and findings updates needed to state that semantic boundary honestly. A standing instruction to continue, finish, or run autonomously authorizes that semantic sequence; it does **not** authorize a new or expanded harness, process layer, evidence framework, protocol, schema, generator, registry, qualification campaign, migration, documentation-governance reorganization, trust mechanism, or other infrastructure initiative.

Before beginning any such harness or governance work, surface it to the user and explain the concrete reproduced need, why the existing mechanisms are insufficient, its expected semantic or consumer payoff, scope and lifecycle cost, alternatives, and what can be avoided. Wait for explicit approval of that work. Do not hide infrastructure inside a semantic capsule or justify speculative machinery as preparation for possible future scale.

Never perform redundant work. Before implementing or proposing a capability, inspect its current owner, code, tests, Git history, active plans, and any in-progress cross-project handoff. Reuse a settled mechanism and wait for an already assigned change instead of cloning, reimplementing, re-verifying, repackaging, or launching a parallel effort. If the requested or planned work appears already satisfied, overlapping, obsolete, or owned by another active agent or project, **surface the exact overlap and block that work** until the user decides whether to no-op, consolidate, replace, or proceed for a stated new reason. Sunk cost is not a reason to retain duplicate machinery, and prior investment is not a reason to create another compatibility layer.

## The source-of-truth hierarchy

Three layers, in authority order for a semantic question:

1. **`../a12-kernel` — the ultimate source of truth (the behavioural oracle).** The real engine. When the local theory and the engine disagree, **the engine wins and the theory is corrected**. Use it to learn and to differentially test — always under the clean-room rule above.
2. **`spec/` and the Lean theory (this repo) — the primary working reference.** The distilled, language-neutral semantics, executable definitions, and proved consequences of the chosen account. The `§n` taxonomy mirrors a12-dmkits, so section numbers line up across repos.
3. **a12-dmkits — the knowledge source, checked out locally as `../a12-rulekit/`.** A peer **clean-room** reimplementation of the same semantics (in Kotlin), a reusable test corpus, semantics ledgers, and an evaluation harness. Its public release is **dmtool-release**.

## Building & running

Toolchain: **Lean 4.31.0**, pinned in [`lean-toolchain`](lean-toolchain); Lake 5.0. There are no external Lake package dependencies. Native executables still incorporate pinned toolchain/runtime components, including GMP; [`docs/PRODUCTION-RELEASE.md`](docs/PRODUCTION-RELEASE.md) owns their release implications.

```sh
lake build                          # build definitions, proofs, and executable conformance locks
lake test                           # replay the 51 compact non-public kernel projections
lake exe checkReferenceProcess      # check the CLI, fixtures, manifest, 25 public evidence associations, and candidate runner
lake exe checkBoundedProcess        # check timeout, streamed caps, exact bytes, and process-group cleanup
lake exe checkCandidateConformance --self-test --suite reference/single-group-correlation-v2.conformance.json
lake exe checkCandidateConformance --self-test --suite reference/flat-validation-empty-logic-v2.conformance.json
lake exe checkCandidateConformance --candidate .lake/build/bin/a12-kernel-reference --suite reference/single-group-correlation-v2.conformance.json

./scripts/check-lean-trust.sh       # reject proof/runtime escape hatches and audit theorem-root axioms
lake env lean A12Kernel/Core.lean   # elaborate a single module with imports available
```

The canonical red/green workflow, executable-example conventions, proof/trust boundary, external replay method, and final gate are documented in [`docs/TESTING.md`](docs/TESTING.md). Follow it for every semantic capsule.

## Conventions

- **English is canonical** — code, identifiers, comments, docs — matching the A12 OSS family.
- **Lean comments:** Use `/-! ... -/` for a nontrivial module's purpose, semantic scope, and boundary; `/-- ... -/` for public semantic declarations and main theorems; and `--` only beside a non-obvious implementation or proof choice. Following a12-dmkits' intent, explain rationale, phase or context, an invariant or counterexample, and a short provenance pointer (`spec/` section, finding, or evidence ID) when those facts are not evident from the types or statements. Never narrate Lean syntax, restate an encoded definition or truth table, document routine proof steps, or let prose claim more than the formal statement. Keep durable analysis in [`docs/`](docs/); code comments stay concise, local, and synchronized with tests and proofs.
- **Commits:** Conventional Commits (`type(scope): subject`, imperative, lowercase type); subject-only by default, a 1–2 sentence body only for a non-obvious *why*.
- Keep this file tool-neutral; [`AGENTS.md`](AGENTS.md) is a symlink to it so Codex and other AGENTS.md-reading agents get the same guidance.
