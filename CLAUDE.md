# CLAUDE.md

Guidance for working in **a12-kernel-lean** — a clean-room, versioned **Lean 4 formal theory and executable specification** of the A12 kernel's validation & computation semantics.

## What this is

A12 Kernel is mgm technology partners' model-and-DSL engine for complex business forms: business analysts declare **validation rules** (each states the *error* condition — true ⇒ the data is invalid) and **computations** (derived fields) in a bilingual EN/DE DSL, and the engine evaluates them against form *Documents*. This project preserves that observed **evaluation semantics** as a versioned mechanized theory: executable as a reference oracle, empirically anchored to kernel 30.8.1, equipped with a required proof spine plus selectively proved higher-level properties, and designed to produce purpose-specific semantic shipments for independent evaluators and later checked consumers such as importers and rule-refactoring tools.

The project-owned language-neutral semantics live in [`spec/`](spec/) — start at [`spec/SEMANTICS-MAP.md`](spec/SEMANTICS-MAP.md) and follow the numbered deep-dives. They are the semantic bridge to a12-dmkits. [`docs/README.md`](docs/README.md) is the sole registry and ownership map for the living project documentation; do not reproduce its complete catalog here.

- **Extend semantics:** start from the canonical clause in [`spec/`](spec/), use [`docs/SOURCES.md`](docs/SOURCES.md) for its provenance and drill path, record every locally originated behavioral spec change that still needs peer reconciliation in the same change under [`docs/A12-DMKITS-SPEC-SYNC-LEDGER.md`](docs/A12-DMKITS-SPEC-SYNC-LEDGER.md), inspect current clause/proof/evidence coverage in [`docs/IMPLEMENTATION-MAP.md`](docs/IMPLEMENTATION-MAP.md), and follow the capsule workflow in [`docs/TESTING.md`](docs/TESTING.md). An inbound correction already committed and reviewed in a12-dmkits is recorded as provenance, not re-issued as an outbound ledger request. [`spec/13-lean-encoding-guide.md`](spec/13-lean-encoding-guide.md) remains consulted staged guidance, not a writable plan.
- **Resume current work:** read [`docs/PLAN.md`](docs/PLAN.md) for the verified baseline, active semantic unit, next consumer probe, and stop conditions; follow its links to the owning evidence and capability records.
- **Explore a consumer:** start with the bounded probe catalog in [`docs/USE-CASES.md`](docs/USE-CASES.md), then use [`docs/IMPLEMENTER-GUIDE.md`](docs/IMPLEMENTER-GUIDE.md) for the task profile, probe boundary, semantic shipments, and independent-consumer gates. Use [`docs/PROTOCOL.md`](docs/PROTOCOL.md) only when the selected task actually consumes the normalized reference process.

## Goal & role

**The primary goal is eventual 100% semantic conformance with A12 kernel 30.8.1 across its complete observable validation-and-computation language and required static legality.** Supported fragments are honest intermediate checkpoints, never an acceptable permanent scope ceiling. Reach that target as a versioned mechanized theory, executable-first, with a required proof spine and additional proofs selected by payoff. Build small semantic capsules: each lands with its executable clause, useful law when one exists, nearest checked non-law, exact assumptions, coverage entry, and honest external-evidence status. Exercise closed material through bounded consumer probes across the Execute/Translate/Transform/Compile/Analyze/Verify/Synthesize/Qualify/Explain/Govern taxonomy owned by [`docs/PRODUCT-PROPOSAL.md`](docs/PRODUCT-PROPOSAL.md#general-consumer-task-categories) and explained for users in [`docs/USE-CASES.md`](docs/USE-CASES.md); probes test whether the semantic representation transports and serves real tasks, but never justify leaving kernel behavior unmodeled. Promote only explicitly adopted, research-closed capabilities into versioned purpose-specific semantic shipments. Selected semantic families are differentially calibrated against compact retained observations from the real engine; internally closed families may remain honestly marked `external evidence pending` until a coherent calibration batch. Proofs establish internal universal consequences and semantic-preservation bridges, never universal correspondence to the external kernel or automatic correctness of an independent consumer. Lean is the ecosystem's formal semantics-of-record for the chosen account of observed behaviour—not a replacement for the shipped Kotlin interpreter. Read [`docs/PROJECT-DESIGN.md`](docs/PROJECT-DESIGN.md) for the charter and [`docs/LEAN-FORMALIZATION.md`](docs/LEAN-FORMALIZATION.md) before extending the proof or semantics architecture; read [`docs/ARCHITECTURE.md`](docs/ARCHITECTURE.md) before extending the core types.

## Semantic-first simplification rule

The following permanent semantic-throughput rules govern all future work:

1. Build high-connectivity keystone mechanisms first when their prerequisites and discriminators are ready: the shared checked expression tree and evaluator; reusable resolved operand streams for iteration, filtering, and correlation; computation scheduling and state transition; repeatable addressing and scope resolution; a temporal profile for model-zone calendar behavior; and message construction and interpolation. Once a mechanism is right, close individual operators as small declarations and specialized laws rather than new architectures.

2. Audit related operator families in batches. Produce one semantic decision table, identify the common mechanism and true deviations, implement the common core once, and then instantiate every closed member.

3. Track five completion levels independently: internally executable and tested; important laws and non-laws proved; kernel correspondence retained; public protocol exposed; and consumer-probe qualified. Ordinary semantic work should normally stop after the first two levels until a coherent family milestone justifies later work.

4. Prove generic scan, verdict, observation, poison, selection, and transition laws once at their owning mechanism. Derive operator-specific results by `simp`, rewriting, or specialization wherever their meanings actually agree; do not accumulate theorem restatements distinguished only by an operator name.

5. Reduce conformance to a separating matrix. Every retained case must distinguish a realistic wrong account such as wrong order, empty substitution, polarity, unknown collapse, scope or row selection, or poison timing. Keep one representative per independent branch, and target mutations at semantic seams without creating a mutation framework.

6. Keep ordinary documentation changes to [`docs/IMPLEMENTATION-MAP.md`](docs/IMPLEMENTATION-MAP.md) and [`docs/PLAN.md`](docs/PLAN.md). Change [`docs/LEAN-FINDINGS.md`](docs/LEAN-FINDINGS.md) only for a durable non-obvious lesson and the spec-sync ledger only for a canonical semantic correction or a12-dmkits request; change README, architecture, protocol, evidence, or implementer kits only when their owned public responsibility changes.

7. Batch external evidence by coherent family. Internally closed clauses may remain honestly marked `external evidence pending`; do not request a kernel capture for each small operator.

8. Run bounded consumer probes only at capability milestones: after a reusable family closes, after a major addressing or computation boundary changes, or before declaring a public compatibility capability. A probe tests knowledge transport and does not become a permanent lockstep product suite by default.

9. Interleave breadth with early high-risk spikes. Establish a minimal foundation in each major area, then test its most dangerous uncertainty before broadening routine operators—for example model-zone discontinuities, multi-level outer correlation, scheduling/poison/clearing, and enumeration remapping or locale collisions.

10. Maintain a strict semantic-work ratio: at least three substantial semantic work units must land between infrastructure or product-boundary units. Aim for 70–80% semantics and proofs, 10–20% focused source audit and review, and under 10% documentation and settled gates. Any new harness, generator, schema, protocol, governance mechanism, or dependency requires a concrete repeated problem, an explanation of need and alternatives, and explicit user approval.

> **Permanent rolling-consolidation rule:** Consolidation is performed locally as each semantic family is extended. Do not pause semantic progress for repository-wide proof or test cleanup, and do not generalize until a second completed semantic user proves that the mechanism is genuinely shared.

For each family, inventory existing ASTs, evaluators, result types, proofs, and conformance cases before writing new representations. Separate genuine duplication from intentional semantic differences, extract a shared function or theorem only when at least two completed semantic users have the same meaning and result domain, restate member theorems as specializations, and remove a case only after another retained case demonstrably guards the same branch. Reuse an existing type only when its current meaning fits exactly.

> **Permanent consumer-adequacy rule:** Lean code, proofs, and green conformance cases are not sufficient completion by themselves. Design and review every semantic representation against at least one concrete consumer task from [`docs/USE-CASES.md`](docs/USE-CASES.md). It must retain the distinctions that task needs, expose or support its decision procedure or preservation relation, and let a future shipment close the task without renewed kernel archaeology or semantic guessing. If a finding changes those needs, feed it back into the representation, proof obligations, separating matrix, exclusions, and plan before extending the family. Fix an inadequate semantic root rather than compensating with consumer-specific adapter logic.

The default unit of closure is an A12 semantic capsule with compact conformance cases and payoff-selected laws, not a new evidence or process layer. Apply a live-value test before retaining non-semantic code: name its current consumer, the unique invariant it enforces, and why a smaller existing mechanism cannot enforce that invariant. If any answer is missing, surface it as a consolidation or retirement candidate; deleting or migrating retained infrastructure still requires the explicit approval below. Previous implementation cost, historical reproducibility, and possible future reuse are not live value; preserve completed outcomes as compact immutable artifacts and exact Git revision pointers instead of runnable legacy machinery.

The default Tier 1 capsule touch budget prefers existing family owners and permits at most one new semantic/proof/conformance trio when a distinct responsibility warrants it, plus small updates to existing document owners, at most 250 new nonblank Lean lines, and zero new IO executables, schemas, receipts, registries, generators, or packet binders. The line target is a decomposition signal, not permission to compress readable code: exceed it only after splitting by semantic responsibility or recording why the semantic mechanism is genuinely indivisible.

A capsule may land as `external evidence pending`. Consume compact retained observations or a newly accepted purpose-specific handback; do not assume a standing producer contract. Build cross-family infrastructure once at a stable boundary and then treat it as settled. Reopen infrastructure only when an actual consumer is blocked or a second semantic family demonstrates the same unmet need; never for anticipated reuse. New shared infrastructure requires a demonstrated repeated need that the settled compact path cannot serve, a named semantic consumer, and an explicit deletion or replacement plan for superseded code.

Apply a live-value test before retaining process or evidence source: it stays only when current semantics or a current consumer depends on it, it guards a failure class not already owned by a smaller settled mechanism, or it is needed to reproduce non-frozen current state. Immutable evidence and historical reports are not live source consumers. Once their exact bytes and last reproducing revision are recorded and a current gate pins the accepted artifacts, surface completed one-off generators, adapters, packet binders, differential campaigns, and qualification runners as retirement candidates and obtain approval before deleting or migrating them. Git history is the reproduction archive; prior effort and possible future convenience are not retention arguments.

The bounded simplification wave is complete. Treat the common evidence and process boundary as settled and resume from the first unfinished semantic step in [`docs/PLAN.md`](docs/PLAN.md). Reopen infrastructure only for a reproduced defect in a retained shared mechanism, a genuinely new observation shape required by an adopted semantic family, or a concrete shipment/consumer gate. Routine capsules add no process format or qualification layer. If capsule-specific support code approaches or exceeds its semantics, proofs, and conformance code, stop and redesign around the compact shared path before continuing.

The repository-level ratio is also a guard. Count `Core.lean`, `Cell.lean`, `Document.lean`, `Basic.lean`, the `Semantics/`, `Elaboration/`, `Proofs/`, and `Conformance/` trees, and their library roots as the executable theory estate. Count the `Evidence/`, `Reference/`, and `Process/` trees plus their IO/test drivers as the support estate. Support must not exceed theory in tracked nonblank Lean lines, and evidence alone should remain below one third of theory. Crossing either threshold requires stopping, reporting the concrete consumer and unique invariant causing it, and redesigning or obtaining an explicit owner exception before adding more support code. The completed count method and migration measurements remain in the [archived simplification work program](docs/archived/REFERENCE-AND-EVIDENCE-SIMPLIFICATION-PROPOSAL.md); the live scaling rule and settled baseline are owned by the [`compact semantic-evidence pipeline`](docs/SEMANTIC-CAPSULE-PIPELINE-PROPOSAL.md).

## ⚠️ HARD RULE — clean-room reimplementation; never link, call, or transcribe the kernel

This is a **licensing boundary, not a preference**, and it is the same rule the a12-dmkits interpreter already operates under. The A12 kernel is **EUPL-1.2 (copyleft) OR commercial**. A work that *links, ships, or calls* the kernel is a combined work the EUPL copyleft reaches; a **line-by-line transliteration of its source** is a copyright derivative that re-attracts the EUPL. Therefore:

- **Never** link, call, or ship the kernel (or a JS-kernel binding) from this project, and **never** transcribe a kernel *source expression* into Lean.
- **Do** read the kernel — and the a12-dmkits interpreter under `../a12-rulekit/interpreter/` — to *learn the exact behaviour*, then write **original** Lean and lock its chosen account with focused examples and laws; replay matching retained differentials when available and otherwise record `external evidence pending`. Reading the source to *understand* (and to find surprising special cases) is encouraged; the *fix* is never a transliteration. **Copy the mechanism, never the expression.**

Because this project's source ships no kernel code, it is licensed **MIT** (see [`LICENSE`](LICENSE)) — matching the A12 OSS family (a12-dmkits source is MIT for the same reason; see [`../a12-rulekit/docs/LICENSING.md`](../a12-rulekit/docs/LICENSING.md)).

## The source-of-truth hierarchy

Three layers, in authority order for a semantic question:

1. **`../a12-kernel` — the ultimate source of truth (the behavioural oracle).** The real engine. When the local theory and the engine disagree, **the engine wins and the theory is corrected**. Use it to learn and to differentially test — always under the clean-room rule above.
2. **`spec/` and the Lean theory (this repo) — the primary working reference.** The distilled, language-neutral semantics, executable definitions, and proved consequences of the chosen account. The `§n` taxonomy mirrors a12-dmkits, so section numbers line up across repos.
3. **a12-dmkits — the knowledge source, checked out locally as `../a12-rulekit/`.** A peer **clean-room** reimplementation of the same semantics (in Kotlin), a reusable test corpus, semantics ledgers, and an evaluation harness. Its public release is **dmtool-release**.

## Building & running

Native executables still incorporate pinned toolchain/runtime components, including GMP; [`docs/PRODUCTION-RELEASE.md`](docs/PRODUCTION-RELEASE.md) owns their release implications.

```sh
lake build                          # build definitions, proofs, and executable conformance locks

./scripts/check-lean-trust.sh       # reject proof/runtime escape hatches and audit theorem-root axioms
lake env lean A12Kernel/Core.lean   # elaborate a single module with imports available
```

## Layout and dependency spine

- [`A12Kernel.lean`](A12Kernel.lean) — library root for the trusted semantics, proofs, and in-library conformance modules identified below; process and evidence drivers remain separate.
- [`A12Kernel/Core.lean`](A12Kernel/Core.lean) — the truth/polarity algebra and value domain: `K` (strong-Kleene, no negation), `Polarity`, `Verdict` + `conj`/`disj`, signed-or-unknown `ScaleInfo`, `NumField`, decoded `DateParts`/`TimeOfDay`, exact epoch-millisecond `Instant`, closed `TemporalValue` with calendar provenance, shared `TemporalComponents`, and heterogeneous `Value`.
- [`A12Kernel/Cell.lean`](A12Kernel/Cell.lean) — the phase-sensitive cell model: `FormalCause`, `Phase`, `CheckedCell`, `CellObservation` (empty ≠ invalid, refined into a phase-indexed read).
- [`A12Kernel/Document.lean`](A12Kernel/Document.lean) — addressing & instance: `RowAddr`/`CellAddr`, `Document` (instantiated rows kept separate from cell values), `Env`, and `World` (injected exact clock plus Base Year).
- [`A12Kernel/Semantics/`](A12Kernel/Semantics/) owns pure executable clauses and reusable result domains. Exact family ownership, coverage, evidence, and exclusions belong only in [`docs/IMPLEMENTATION-MAP.md`](docs/IMPLEMENTATION-MAP.md).
- [`A12Kernel/Elaboration/`](A12Kernel/Elaboration/) owns model checking, admitted surface subsets, static legality, and one-pass lowering into executable clauses. Checked wrappers certify their stated subset; they do not imply parser correctness or unrestricted surface acceptance.
- [`A12Kernel/Proofs.lean`](A12Kernel/Proofs.lean) is the trusted theorem root. - Preserve exact decimals and every specified rounding stage; never replace ordered evaluation with algebraic reassociation.
- Keep instantiated repeatable rows separate from cell storage, and keep captured outer environments separate from candidate inner environments.
- Keep validation's extensional unknown/polarity account separate from computation's ordered poison and short-circuit account.

A spike begins from competing accounts and a separating witness only when existing sources or observations can choose an account; otherwise record the unresolved discriminator once and write no Lean. It must end as a closed capsule, a precise unresolved boundary with experimental code removed, or a representation correction with affected gates and theorem statements re-audited. Authorization to perform a semantic spike never authorizes harness or governance work. Implement the first settled instance directly; generalize only after a second completed semantic user demonstrates the same meaning, result domain, and law. The **twelve encoding traps** (encoding guide §2) are where naive attempts silently diverge.

A source-grounded clause may remain `external evidence pending`, and every dependent correspondence claim inherits that status. A representation-affecting unresolved alternative is different: dependent work on that branch is blocked. A probe authorizes no external prototype, dependency, protocol, shipment, runner, qualification campaign, or product work; surface and obtain approval for any such expansion under the infrastructure rule above.

## Conventions

- **English is canonical** — code, identifiers, comments, docs — matching the A12 OSS family.
- **Lean comments:** Use `/-! ... -/` for a nontrivial module's purpose, semantic scope, and boundary; `/-- ... -/` for public semantic declarations and main theorems; and `--` only beside a non-obvious implementation or proof choice. Following a12-dmkits' intent, explain rationale, phase or context, an invariant or counterexample, and a short provenance pointer (`spec/` section, finding, or evidence ID) when those facts are not evident from the types or statements. Never narrate Lean syntax, restate an encoded definition or truth table, document routine proof steps, or let prose claim more than the formal statement. Keep durable analysis in [`docs/`](docs/); code comments stay concise, local, and synchronized with tests and proofs.
- **Commits:** Conventional Commits (`type(scope): subject`, imperative, lowercase type); subject-only by default, a 1–2 sentence body only for a non-obvious *why*.
- Keep this file tool-neutral; [`AGENTS.md`](AGENTS.md) is a symlink to it so Codex and other AGENTS.md-reading agents get the same guidance.
